Rock Climbing

This year (2019) I started training early in the season hoping to hop on the infamous Yosemite climb, astroman. I've been focusing on getting good at crack climbing, which is a difficult style to learn.

Here I am toproping the crux of hotrocks, an 11.b finger crack.(Beta Alert!) This crack is particularly difficult because it is not a straight forward crack. There are many features outside the crack itself that must be used as well as friction against the sides of the wall.
The size of the crack varies with some being a very awkward size such as this "offwidth" crack. Here, you can't quite jam your whole body in the crack and the crack too large to get a constriction around your hand, which make this technique the most strenuous form of climbing, at least for me!
